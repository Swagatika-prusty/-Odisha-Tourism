The Jagannath Temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, believed to be an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, originally built in 1198 BCE. The temple is not open to non-Hindus and is surrounded by two walls and a 190-foot Shikhara which is mounted by Vishnu’s discus-shaped Sudarshan Chakra. The presiding deity of the temple, Lord Jagannath is depicted in black with large white eyes, the idol being tended to regularly by changing its clothes. The temple also houses idols of Lord Jagannath’s sister Subhadra and brother Balbhadra.
The entry to the temple is free and jagannath temple darshan timings 05:00 am and 10:00 pm, offering free darshan to all devotees. But if you wish to make a special puja booking, you can do so directly off the temple ticket counter.
RATHA YATRA : The Festival of Chariots: Chariots of Shri Jagannath is celebrated every year at Puri, the temple town in Odisha, on the second (dwitiya) day of shukla pakshya (waxing cycle of moon) of Āshādha Māsa (3rd month of Odia calendar).







Puri is in the coastal delta of the Mahanadi River on the shores of the Bay of Bengal. In the ancient days it was near to Sisupalgarh (also known as "Ashokan Tosali"). Then the land was drained by a tributary of the Bhargavi River, a branch of the Mahanadi River. This branch underwent a meandering course creating many arteries altering the estuary, and formed many sand hills. These sand hills could be cut through by the streams. Because of the sand hills, the Bhargavi River, flowing to the south of Puri, moved away towards the Chilika Lake.
The beach at Puri, known as the "Ballighai beach, at the mouth of Nunai River", is 8 kilometres (5.0 mi) away from the town and is fringed by casurina trees.It has golden yellow sand. Sunrise and sunset are pleasant scenic attractions here.Waves break in at the beach which is long and wide.






Shopping in the bazaars of Puri is definitely an experience. One would simply find delight in bargaining for the variety of products in here. There are different bazaars set across Puri meant for various reasons. If one is looking to buy traditional sarees in Puri, one should pay a visit to Swargadwar which is right opposite Puri Hotel. Here one would find beautiful and exotic traditional sarees.
This place is often the center of attraction for tourists of Puri. It is indeed the best shopping experience. If one is looking to buy patta chitra then one should visit the Boyanika near the Jagannath temple, Utkalika near Puri hotel or the Orissa Emporium. These are the most sought after places for buying Patta Chitra.
Traditional sarees like Passapali, Bomkai, Ikat as well as Sambalpuri are available for shopping in this indulgent and holy town of Puri. Palm leaf painting is another shopping delight offered by this beautiful town of Puri. Patta Chitra as it is called is a famous art form of Odisha.
