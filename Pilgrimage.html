PURI TEMPLE

Address :Puri(jagannath temple), Odisha 752001.
Timing:Open(5AM-10PM)
Established : 12th century (Ananta Varman Chodaganga Deva).
Festivals :Ratha Yatra , Chandan Yatra, Snana Yatra, Nabakalebara
Elevation :65 m (213 ft)
Management :Shree Jagannath Temple Office, Puri, Odisha, Shree Jagannath Temple Managing Committee, Puri.

The Jagannath Temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, believed to be an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, originally built in 1198 BCE. The temple is not open to non-Hindus and is surrounded by two walls and a 190-foot Shikhara which is mounted by Vishnu’s discus-shaped Sudarshan Chakra.
The entry to the temple is free and jagannath temple darshan timings 05:00 am and 10:00 pm, offering free darshan to all devotees.
RATHA YATRA : The Festival of Chariots: Chariots of Shri Jagannath is celebrated every year at Puri, the temple town in Odisha, on the second (dwitiya) day of shukla pakshya (waxing cycle of moon) of Āshādha Māsa (3rd month of Odia calendar).


LINGARAJ TEMPLE


Address : Lingaraj Ample, Lingaraj Temple Rd, Old Town, Bhubaneswar, Odisha 751002
Timing:24 HOURS
Established :King Jajati Keshari in the 10th Century and completed by King Lalatendu Keshari in the 11th Century.
Height: 55 m
Management :governed by the Odisha Hindu Religious Endowment Act, 1951, Government of Odisha.

The imposing temple standing at 180 ft strikes awe and respect amongst its visitors. Built during the 11th century AD. The temple has been described as “One of the finest examples of purely Hindu Temple in India” by noted critic and historian James Fergusson (1808-1886).
The other attraction of the temple is the Bindusagar Lake, located in the north side of the temple.
Shivaratri : held in the month of Feb/March : a festival where a woman fasts and prays for the long life of her husband and Rukuna Ratha Yatra held on Ashokastami . Rukuna Ratha Yatra : literally means the chariot which doesnot take a U-turn. Both these festivities draw huge crowds to the temple.


MUKTESWARA TEMPLE


Address : Old Town, Bhubaneswar, Odisha 751019
Timing:7AM-7PM
Established :10th Century AD
Architectural style: Kalinga architecture
Elevation : 2171 m (7,123 ft)
Management : Government of Odisha.

The Mukteshvara Temple is found to be the earliest work from the Somavamshi period. Most scholars believe the temple is the successor to Parashurameshvara Temple and built earlier to the Brahmeswara Temple (1060 CE).
Porch :The most important feature of the Mukteshvara Temple is the torana, or the arched gateway, dating back to about 900 CE and showing the influence of Buddhist architecture.
Vimana: The Vimana is square in plan and is built in a raised platform with pilasters in each facade. Sanctum : The sanctum is sculpted with beautiful damsels exhibiting feminine charms entwined with nagas and naginis.


GHATGAON TEMPLE


Address :Ghatgaon,Kendujhar, Odisha 758027.
Hours :Open(7AM-5PM)
Festivals : Chaiti Yatra ,Patua Yatra,Ashadhi parba
Established :1480
Elevation : 330 m (1,080 ft)
Management : Government of Odisha.

Maa Tarini Temple: It is the main shrine of Maa Tarini who is regarded as the embodiments of Shakti. She is one of the chief presiding Goddess in Odia culture.
By road : Buses are always available from many towns of Odisha like Kendujhar, Bhubaneswar. By rail :Nearest railway station is Harichandanpur railway station which is 16 km away. By air :Nearest airport is Biju Patnaik Airport at Bhubaneswar which is 174 km away.
Chaiti Yatra is the most popular and famous festival of this place celebrated in the month of April. The first day of Baisakha is treated as the Odia New Year (ଓଡ଼ିଆ ନବବର୍ଷ)। Patua Yatra is another most famous festival of this place celebrated in the month of April. Ashadhi parba (ଆଷାଢ଼ି ପର୍ବ) is also Celebrated here in the last Thursday of the Odia Month: Asadha (ଆଷାଢ଼ ମାସ).
