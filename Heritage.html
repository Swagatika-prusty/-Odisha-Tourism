KHANDAGIRI

Address :Bhubaneswar, Odisha 751030.
Hours :9AM-6PM
Established :1st century BCE.
Area :6.13 km2.
Management :cultural minister, Government of Odisha.

The caves located 7KM from Bhubaneswar takes us back in time and is probably the only recorded history of era that is still shrouded in mystery. Built somewhere around the 2nd century BC by King Kharavela of the Meghavahana dynasty, these ancient rock cut caves were first discovered by a young British Officer Andrew Sterling in the 19th century AD.
Hathigumpha : or the elephant cave as its known bears the inscriptions of King Kharavela. The seventeen lines mentions about his many conquests and his way of governance. The Rani Gumpha : or the Queen cave ; is a double storeyed structure with beautiful carvings. Exquisite carvings apart, the cave is known for its acoustic characteristics.
The Ganesh Gumpha : the Ganesha Cave known for its carvings of Jain teerthankar’s and other sculptures. The carving of Lord Ganesha and two elephants were added much later.



UDAYAGIRI


Address :Bhubaneswar, Odisha 751030.
Hours :9AM-6PM
Established :1st century BCE.
Area :7.13 km2.
Management :cultural minister, Government of Odisha.

Udayagiri is a ticketed site and is under heritage site is well guarded and maintained by ASI (Archaeological Survey of India). As you enter the cave complex you can go in various directions as the caves are all spread out. The numbering of the caves is also not in any particular order. The map at the entrance gives you the position of caves but it’s a bit tricky to find out their exact location in reality.
Rani Gumpha :To reach this cave you have to take the right path as you enter Udayagiri. This is the largest cave complex and one of the most beautiful ones. This cave has multiple sections and is on two floors.
Chotho Hanthi Gumpha :As the name suggests this cave has an elephant cut out on the front. The single cave has a huge rock bolder on top. Pillars were later added to support the top weight. Alkapuri Gumpha :This is a double-storied cave. The lower section is simple with just one chamber and two stone pillars. The upper section is single-chambered with stone pillars.



RATNAGIRI

Address :Jajpur, Odisha 754292.
Hours :Open(9AM-5PM)
Established :5th century.
Area :7.13 km2.
Management :cultural minister, Government of Odisha.

Ratnagiri (Odia: ରତ୍ନଗିରି, meaning "hill of jewels") is the site of a ruined mahavihara, once the major Buddhist monastery in modern Odisha, India. It is located on a hill in between the Brahmani and Birupa rivers in Jajpur district. It is close to other Buddhist sites in the area, including Lalitagiri and Udayagiri, and 100 km (62 mi) from the state capital Bhubaneswar and 70km from the former state capital Cuttack.

Monastery :Monastery 1 is much the largest of the three monasteries, with an overall size of 55 square metres, including 21 square metres for the paved central courtyard.
Stupas and temples :The main stupa dates to the 9th century and was likely built on the site of an earlier, Gupta-era stupa. It is on the highest point in the site and has a square base, 47 feet (14 m) metres on each side.



DHAULIGIRI

Address :Jajpur, Odisha 754292.
Hours :Open(9AM-5PM)
Established :5th century.
Area :7.13 km2.
Management :cultural minister, Government of Odisha.

With the beautiful Daya River lying in the proximity, you have the ancient land of Dhauli that lets you know about the enormous battles that were fought here, the Kalinga War of 261 B.C. Buddhism for which Dhauli is so well known played a crucial role in transforming King Ashoka to a diehard Buddha devotee who eventually followed the preaching of Buddha for the rest of his life.
Dhauli is famous for the edicts related to Buddhism that were composed from the Brahmi script and used Prakrit language. One can spot a series of rock cut monuments at this hill.
An enormous temple in Dhauli is the Dhavaleswar Temple, which is much frequented by the visitors. In addition to this, you have the Bahirangeswar Temple Siva Temple as well as the Ganesha Temple that together add to the religious aspect to this place.
